Where No One Counts

  • From the US-Mexico crossing to the Sahara’s heat
  • The Mediterranean waves, where destinies meet
  • Through deserts and seas, across invisible lines they roam
  • A journey of many, yet none find their home
  • They walked beneath the burning sun
  • Feet on dust, with nowhere to run
  • Chasing a dream, a sliver of light
  • A place to rest, an end to the night
  • They left behind stories
  • Wrapped in the arms of mothers
  • Pressed into the palms of fathers
  • Tucked inside the laughter of brothers
  • They crossed rivers with no bridge in sight
  • Boarded boats in the dead of night
  • Waded through deserts that swallowed their names
  • Stepped onto roads where none remain
  • But the tide rose higher than their cries
  • The sun burned fierce beneath their eyes
  • The border stretched beyond their will
  • And silence waits for them still
  • The border counted them once
  • Trespassers. Statistics. Infractions.
  • A file opened, a number assigned
  • A case closed before the ink had dried
  • The sea took them in
  • No names. No papers. No protest.
  • No appeals could be chased
  • No fingerprints could be traced
  • A boat overturned, but the logs show none
  • A child disappeared, but the ledger reads one
  • A man begged for water, but the form was denied
  • A body was buried, but no one cried
  • We do not count them unless they belong
  • We do not grieve them unless they are strong
  • We do not mourn them unless they exist
  • And so they vanish, dismissed
  • Vanished once, where no one can see
  • Vanished twice, from history
  • Stamped and erased
  • Filed and replaced
  • But we will not move on
  • We will not forget the hands that reached for a future
  • And were met with waves that did not care
  • Gates that did not open
  • Land that did not dare
  • We will carve their names into the sky
  • Let the stars hold them high
  • Let the earth remember their weight
  • Let the wind echo their fate
  • So even if they are lost
  • They are never truly gone.
